Our Approach

Coaching Philosophy

We begin from the premise that every speaker we work with already has everything they need. Our role is not to add material — it is to remove what is obscuring it.

The most common failure mode in high-stakes speaking is not nerves or poor delivery. It is a lack of conceptual clarity — the inability to reduce a complex, important idea to its essential, communicable core. That is what we work on first.

Once the idea is clear, delivery follows naturally. Structure, presence, pace, and language are all in service of the idea — not the other way around. This is the sequencing that separates a lasting talk from a forgettable performance.

Phase One
Idea Excavation

Finding the precise, singular idea worth sharing — often buried beneath years of expertise, accumulated knowledge, and professional caution.

Phase Two
Structure & Narrative

Building the talk architecture — the through-line that carries an audience from unfamiliarity to conviction without losing them along the way.

Phase Three
Delivery & Presence

Preparing the body, voice, and mind for performance under pressure — with the confidence that comes from total command of one's material.

The Process

The Speaker Preparation Journey

Upon Acceptance
Welcome & Initial Briefing

Each accepted speaker receives a detailed programme briefing, is introduced to their assigned coach, and begins the preliminary process of idea identification — often the most important work of the entire preparation.

4 Months Before Event
Idea Development Sessions

Three to five structured coaching sessions — conducted remotely — focused on excavating and defining the single idea the speaker will bring to the stage. This phase concludes with a written idea brief reviewed by the lead coach.

2 Months Before Event
Talk Drafting & Structure

The speaker develops the full talk script and structure in close collaboration with their coach. Multiple drafts are reviewed. The talk is stress-tested for clarity, evidence, and emotional resonance.

4 Weeks Before Event
Delivery Rehearsals

Full-length rehearsals — delivered to camera — with specific feedback on pace, tone, movement, eye contact, and the handling of difficult transitions. Speakers record themselves and submit recordings for coach review.

Day Two, Abuja
Live Mastermind

The in-person culmination of months of preparation. Speakers deliver to peers and coaches in a structured live session — receiving final, precise refinements before the main stage on Day Five.
