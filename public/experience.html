September 21–25, 2026

Five Days.
One Defining
Statement.

Each day of the Project TEDxAfrica programme is designed with a specific purpose. Nothing is incidental. From the executive welcome on Day One to the global broadcast on Day Five, the progression is deliberate and cumulative.

01
September 21 — Monday

Arrival & Executive Welcome

The programme opens with a carefully orchestrated executive welcome — not a casual mixer, but a formal introduction that establishes the tone and gravity of what follows.

Speakers arrive at curated accommodation in Abuja and are welcomed into an intimate private dinner with select government representatives, platform leadership, and senior partners. First conversations begin here. Connections that will matter for years are established in these first hours.

Arrival and accommodation orientation
Official registration and programme briefing
Private executive welcome dinner
Programme overview and coaching introductions
02
September 22 — Tuesday

Speaker Mastermind

The full day is dedicated to intensive, structured speaker development. This is the intellectual core of the programme — where ideas are stress-tested, refined, and prepared for the global stage.

Working in small cohorts with experienced coaches, each speaker works through their talk structure, narrative arc, and delivery. Speakers also observe and critically support each other — building the kind of honest, high-level peer review that elevates every participant's output.

Morning: Talk structure workshop with lead coaches
Afternoon: Peer review sessions and one-to-one coaching
Evening: Leadership dialogue — panel with senior policymakers
03
September 23 — Wednesday

Cultural & Institutional Immersion

The ideas worth sharing cannot be developed in isolation from the place where they are being shared. Day Three takes speakers into the institutional and cultural fabric of Abuja and Nigeria's capital territory.

Curated visits to governmental buildings, cultural institutions, and civic spaces provide context, material, and the kind of authority that only physical presence can confer. These are not tourism activities. They are intellectual experiences deliberately chosen to deepen each speaker's sense of what Africa's future demands.

Institutional visit: National Assembly, ECOWAS, or African Union representation
Cultural programme: Nigerian arts, architecture, and heritage access
Evening: Speaker documentary session — professional photography and filmed profiles
04
September 24 — Thursday

Production & Impact

The final preparation day. Speakers move into the production environment — the stage, the lights, the live cameras — and deliver their full talk in a controlled rehearsal setting.

This is not a run-through. It is a performance at near-full intensity, reviewed immediately by coaches with precise, actionable feedback. The afternoon is reserved for individual refinement before the evening impact session — a closed gathering where speakers share the broader implications of their ideas with a curated senior audience.

Morning: Full-dress rehearsal on main stage with production team
Afternoon: Final coaching refinements and individual run-throughs
Evening: Impact session — speaker conversations with senior audience
05
September 25 — Friday

The Main TEDx Event

The day that matters. The stage is set, the audience assembled, the cameras live. Every idea developed over the preceding four days meets its moment.

The main TEDxAfrica event is a full-day programme broadcast globally. Speakers take the stage in sequence, each delivering their prepared talk before an audience of invited delegates drawn from government, international institutions, the private sector, and civil society. The talks are recorded and published to the TEDx global archive within days of the event.

Full-day main stage programme
Live global broadcast
Professional recording for TEDx archive publication
Evening: Closing reception with all delegates and partners
